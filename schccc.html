Indian Judiciary System

The judiciary in India is a system of courts that interpret and apply the law. It consists of the Supreme Court, High Courts, and subordinate courts at district and lower levels.

Supreme Court of India

The apex court of the country and the final court of appeal

About the Supreme Court

The Supreme Court of India is the highest judicial court and the final court of appeal under the Constitution of India. It is the highest constitutional court with the power of judicial review.

Established: 28 January 1950
Location: Tilak Marg, New Delhi
Current Chief Justice: Dhananjaya Y. Chandrachud (As of 2023)
Jurisdiction: Original, Appellate, and Advisory

Functions and Powers

The Supreme Court has three types of jurisdictions: original, appellate, and advisory. It safeguards fundamental rights and settles disputes between governments. It has the power to declare any central or state law as unconstitutional.

Composition

The Supreme Court comprises the Chief Justice and 33 other judges appointed by the President of India. Judges retire at the age of 65.

High Courts of India

The highest judicial courts in the states and union territories

About High Courts

High Courts are the highest courts in each state and union territory of India. There are currently 25 High Courts in India. They have jurisdiction over a state, a union territory, or a group of states and union territories.

Civil Courts in India

The subordinate judiciary that handles civil matters

About Civil Courts

Civil Courts deal with disputes between individuals and/or organizations. The district judiciary is the principal court of original jurisdiction in civil matters. The structure of civil courts is hierarchical with the district judge at the top.

Hierarchy of Civil Courts

District Court

The highest court in the district dealing with civil cases. Headed by the District Judge.

Additional District Court

Additional courts established to handle the workload of the District Court.

Court of Civil Judge (Senior Division)

Handles civil cases where the claim exceeds a specified value (varies by state).

Court of Civil Judge (Junior Division)

Handles smaller civil cases with claims below a specified value.

Munsif Court

In some states, handles small civil cases at the lowest level.

Small Causes Court

Special court for small monetary claims and summary suits.

Functions of Civil Courts

Civil Courts deal with matters related to property disputes, rent agreements, divorce cases, contract violations, and other civil matters. They follow the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908.
